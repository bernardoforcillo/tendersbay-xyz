# Italian keyword map

Living doc. Owner: GTM. Scope: organic search (SEO + future content) for the Italian
market (`it-it`). Positioning and terminology law live in
`.claude/memory/landing-page-design.md` — this doc maps search demand onto that
positioning; it does not restate or fork it.

**Method & caveats.** No paid SEO tool access: there are **no search-volume numbers in
this doc**, and none should be added without a sourced tool export. Evidence is
qualitative — SERP composition, competitor titles/H1s, institutional-portal density,
related vocabulary — gathered via web search in July 2026. Searches were run from a
US-based index, so rankings are an approximation of google.it, not a geo-located
Italian SERP; treat ordering as directional. Re-validate quarterly or when a
competitor pivots.

## 1. Seed landscape — how Italian SMEs actually search

### 1.1 Head terms (the demand core)

| Term | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| **bandi di gara** | The canonical noun for tender notices. SERP is wall-to-wall institutional ("Bandi di gara e contratti" is the legally mandated *amministrazione trasparente* section name on every ministry/region/agency site — Interno, MEF, MIT, Agenzia delle Entrate, CNR, Regione Emilia-Romagna all rank). Commercial players fight for scraps below the fold. |
| **gare d'appalto** (also spelled *gare di appalto*) | The process word. SERP mixes the MIT portal (portaleappalti.mit.gov.it) with **informational guides** from training/insurance/consulting sites (Pedago, Unione Professionisti, Tuttocauzioni, Italiafideiussioni) — i.e. this term skews "how does it work", not "show me tenders". |
| **appalti pubblici** | The domain/regulatory word. Attracts editorial and legal content (BibLus/ACCA, Appalti&Contratti, Agenda Digitale). Good modifier root: *appalti pubblici europei*, *ricerca appalti*. |
| **gare pubbliche / gare europee** | Lower-density variants; *gare europee* is the one head-adjacent phrase with an EU-wide meaning — and it is tendersbay's natural home turf (see gaps, §2.3). |

**Trap — "bandi" is ambiguous.** Unqualified *bandi pubblici / bandi aperti / bandi
2026* SERPs are dominated by **grants** (finanziamenti a fondo perduto, contributi,
PNRR calls — Golden Group, Finera, Ticonsiglio own that space), and *bandi di
concorso* means public-employment exams. Always qualify: **bandi di gara**, *bandi
d'appalto*, *bandi di gara pubblici*. Never target bare "bandi".

### 1.2 Institutional vocabulary (navigational gravity wells)

Terms Italian bidders type because the state makes them: **MEPA** / acquistinretepa
(Consip's marketplace; *abilitazione MEPA* is its own how-to sub-cluster), **Consip**,
**ANAC**, **DGUE** (the it-it ESPD acronym — *compilazione DGUE*, *DGUE elettronico*
are evergreen how-to queries with the European Commission's free ESPD service and MIT
guidelines ranking), **CIG** (*codice identificativo gara* — definitional queries;
note the 2024 Codice moved CIG acquisition from the legacy **SIMOG** to the new
piattaforma contratti pubblici / BDNCP, so SIMOG is now legacy vocabulary), **codice
dei contratti pubblici** (D.Lgs. 36/2023), **GURI/GUUE**, **TED**, plus the regional
e-procurement platforms: **Sintel/ARIA** (Lombardia — also used by Veneto, Piemonte,
Liguria, Abruzzo), START (Toscana), Intercent-ER, STELLA, Sardegna CAT. Process
vocabulary that appears in every guide: *stazione appaltante*, *operatore economico*,
*sopra/sotto soglia*, *capitolato*, *RTI*, *avvalimento*, *DURC*,
**aggiudicazione / esiti di gara**.

The fragmentation itself is the searcher's pain — guides literally advise SMEs to
"iscriversi alle piattaforme dei territori in cui si intende operare" (Scudeller).
That pain is tendersbay's pitch.

### 1.3 The AI angle — what "AI + appalti" means today

Critical finding: in mid-2026 the SERP for *intelligenza artificiale appalti
pubblici / bandi di gara* is **regulatory news, not tools**. ANAC's delibere 148 and
153 (April 2026) updated Bandi-Tipo 1 and 2 to require bidders to **declare AI use in
preparing the technical offer** (referencing legge 132/2025 and the EU AI Act
2024/1689) — Appalti&Contratti, Edilportale, Agenda Digitale, AI4Business all cover
it. Two consequences:

1. Tool-intent AI queries (*software gare d'appalto*, *AI per bandi*, *ricerca bandi
   automatica*, *software ufficio gare*, *alert bandi di gara*) are **low-competition
   and up for grabs** — only FareAppalti.AI visibly optimizes for them.
2. There is a newsjacking lane: *dichiarazione uso AI nell'offerta*, *bando tipo ANAC
   intelligenza artificiale* — content that positions tendersbay as AI-native **and**
   compliance-aware.

## 2. Competitor / SERP snapshot (Italy)

### 2.1 Who owns the head terms

- **Institutional (unbeatable, don't try):** portaleappalti.mit.gov.it, every
  *amministrazione trasparente* section, acquistinretepa.it (MEPA/Consip), ANAC,
  ariaspa.it/Sintel + regional platforms, GURI, and **TED** (ted.europa.eu) for
  anything "europeo". TED self-reports ~746,000 notices/year, ~235,000 worth ~€545bn
  (TED "about" page) — a sourced stat usable in content.
- **Legacy commercial alert services (the incumbents):** **Telemat** (DBInformation;
  35 years in market, claims 200k+ tenders/year monitored from ~35k contracting
  authorities — runs Google Ads on head terms), **InfoAppalti**, **InfoBandiPA /
  infobandi.it** (DigitalPA), **Banchedati.biz** (claims 40k bandi/month from 23k
  sources), CercaAppalti, Mondoappalti, DataOpen, Appaltitalia. Their model:
  keyword/region profile → daily email alert. Their copy is functional, zero swagger,
  Italy-only.
- **AI-first:** **FareAppalti.AI** — title "FareAppalti.AI — Intelligenza Artificiale
  per gli Appalti Pubblici", H1 "Prima piattaforma AI per gli appalti in Italia",
  headline claim "Il tuo ufficio gare lavora il 50% più veloce". Features: ricerca
  bandi with profiling, business intelligence, AI document analysis, gara management,
  7-day free trial. Keywords it visibly targets: *bandi, gare, appalti, intelligenza
  artificiale, ufficio gare, gestione gare*.
- **Editorial (own the informational queries):** appaltiecontratti.it (Maggioli),
  BibLus/ACCA, Edilportale, Agenda Digitale, plus consultancy blogs (Scudeller,
  Timone Services, Banchedati's own guide hub).

### 2.2 What the incumbents' positioning tells us

Every Italian commercial player sells **coverage of Italy** (GURI + bollettini
regionali + piattaforme telematiche) and **email alerts**. FareAppalti.AI adds AI
document analysis but stays **Italy-only** ("in Italia" is literally in its H1) and
sells to the existing *ufficio gare* — the team tendersbay's personas don't have.

### 2.3 Gaps for an AI-first, EU-wide product

1. **Nobody owns "gare europee per PMI".** The EU-wide cluster (*gare europee*,
   *appalti europei*, *bandi TED*, *come partecipare ad appalti in Europa*) is held
   by TED itself and thin institutional explainers (Europe Direct Lombardia,
   Your Europe, opificium.eu). No commercial player pitches cross-border tenders to
   Italian SMEs. This is tendersbay's "27 paesi, una ricerca" wedge.
2. **Agents vs. alerts.** Incumbents stop at "we email you the bando"; tendersbay's
   find→prepare→win narrative covers the part after the alert (DGUE, capitolato,
   strategy) that only FareAppalti.AI partially addresses.
3. **DGUE/bureaucracy long-tail** is answered by PDFs, ministry guidelines, and
   consultancy blogs — no product player owns *compilazione DGUE* with a "let the
   agent fill it" answer.
4. **Tool-intent AI queries are nearly empty** (§1.3) — one competitor, no
   entrenched editorial.

## 3. Keyword clusters by intent

Terminology split applies throughout (see landing-page-design memory): the
procurement-correct **"aggiudicata/aggiudicazione"** family belongs in technical/SEO
spots (meta title, hero highlight, headings); the **"vinci/tue"** swagger only in
emotional micro-copy (footer tagline, CTA). Never bare "bandi" (§1.1 trap).

### Cluster A — Tool/transactional (highest priority: buying intent, low competition)

*software gare d'appalto · ricerca bandi di gara · alert bandi di gara · monitoraggio
gare d'appalto · piattaforma ricerca appalti · AI per bandi di gara · intelligenza
artificiale gare d'appalto · software ufficio gare · ricerca bandi automatica*

- **Surface:** landing meta title + meta description (it-it), hero highlight, future
  product/feature pages. This is where "aggiudicata" belongs.
- **Note:** FareAppalti.AI is the only visible organic competitor; Telemat buys ads
  here. Differentiator words to pair: *europee*, *27 paesi*, *agenti AI*.

### Cluster B — Tender-discovery hybrid (head terms + qualifiers)

*bandi di gara [regione] (lombardia, lazio, sicilia…) · gare d'appalto [settore]
(pulizie, edilizia, informatica, ingegneria, trasporti…) · gare aggiudicate / esiti
di gara · bandi di gara aperti oggi*

- **Surface:** long-term — programmatic/SEO landing pages per region/sector once the
  product is live and can back them with real tender data (pre-launch: do not build
  empty doorway pages). Short-term: the search-dock example queries
  (`landing.search.examples`) should read like these real queries.
- **Note:** regional queries resolve today to regional platforms and
  amministrazione-trasparente pages; sector queries to Telemat's category pages
  (e.g. telemat.it/gare-per-categoria/…) — beatable with real data pages later.

### Cluster C — Institutional-navigational (can't outrank, must speak the language)

*MEPA / acquistinretepa · abilitazione MEPA · Sintel / ARIA · TED · ANAC · DGUE ·
compilazione DGUE · CIG cos'è · codice dei contratti pubblici · D.Lgs. 36/2023*

- **Surface:** body copy as **loanwords/acronyms** (it-it already says DGUE — correct
  per the acronym table; keep it), glossary/guide content later. Never try to outrank
  the institution; rank for the *how-to* around it (*come compilare il DGUE*, *come
  abilitarsi al MEPA*) with a "your agent does this" answer.

### Cluster D — Informational beginner (top of funnel, editorial-owned today)

*come partecipare a una gara d'appalto · come funziona una gara d'appalto · requisiti
per partecipare a un bando di gara · RTI raggruppamento temporaneo di imprese ·
avvalimento · documenti per gara d'appalto*

- **Surface:** blog/guides (the footer already promises "Blog"). These queries are
  held by consultancies and training sites, not products — a persona-led guide
  ("partecipare senza ufficio gare") can enter. Tone: cutting toward the rigged
  system, warm toward the reader.

### Cluster E — Emergent AI-regulatory (newsjack lane)

*bando tipo ANAC intelligenza artificiale · dichiarazione uso AI offerta tecnica ·
legge 132/2025 appalti · AI Act appalti pubblici*

- **Surface:** blog/thought-leadership. Positions tendersbay as the AI-native player
  that already answers the compliance question SMEs are about to be asked in every
  gara (the assurance section's Q&A tone extends naturally here).

### Negative keywords (paid + content hygiene)

*fondo perduto · finanziamenti · contributi · bandi di concorso · concorsi pubblici ·
PNRR (unqualified) · aste giudiziarie* — different intents that will pollute any
campaign or content targeting "bandi".

## 4. Mapping to current it-it copy & SEO config

Sources of truth read: `apps/platform/src/assets/locales/it-it/common.json`
(`landing` namespace) and the `seo({...})` block in `apps/platform/vite.config.ts`.

### Already aligned

- **Meta title** "tendersbay — Gare europee, aggiudicate dai tuoi agenti AI": carries
  *gare europee* (the uncontested EU-wide head phrase, §2.3) and procurement-correct
  *aggiudicate* in the technical spot. Correct per the terminology split.
- **Meta description** (it-it) carries *gare pubbliche europee*, *PMI*,
  *aggiudicazione* — good Cluster A/B vocabulary.
- **Body copy** uses *DGUE* (3×, correct it-it acronym), *appalti pubblici europei*
  (vision), *capitolato* (agents), *ufficio gare* (problem — the exact phrase
  FareAppalti.AI sells to). Footer tagline "Le gare d'Europa. Tue." keeps the swagger
  in the emotional slot. Split respected throughout.

### Top opportunities (recommendations only — no edits in this run)

1. **The word "bando/bandi" never appears in the it-it landing namespace.** The
   highest-density Italian head family (*bandi di gara*) is absent from the entire
   page. Lowest-effort fix: work *bandi di gara* into the it-it
   `landing.meta.description` (e.g. "…scovano i bandi di gara e le gare pubbliche
   europee giuste…") and consider it in one on-page technical spot (a problem-card or
   agents-card body). Keep "bandi" always qualified (§1.1 trap). Ships as a 24-locale
   copy change per the standard recipe (each locale using its own head-term
   equivalent), with completeness test.
2. **`landing.meta.description` is authored in all 24 locales but never rendered.**
   `features/landing/components/pages/landing-page/index.tsx` sets `document.title`
   from `landing.meta.title`, but nothing writes the description tag — the only
   description any crawler sees is the static English one injected by
   `@tendersbay/vite-plugin-seo`. Wire the localized description client-side (same
   pattern as the title) so JS-rendering crawlers pick up Italian keywords on
   `/it-it/`.
3. **Per-locale static head is a build-time gap.** The plugin deliberately injects
   one static head for all routes (SPA, no SSR — and no canonical / no canary-noindex
   by design; those decisions stand). Consequence: social scrapers (no JS) show
   English meta + `og:title` = bare "tendersbay" on Italian shares. Candidate
   extension (user decision, touches `packages/vite-plugin-seo`): emit per-locale
   `<head>` variants for the locale-prefixed routes at build, or at minimum set
   `og:title` to the full default-locale meta title instead of `siteName`. Live check
   confirmed the served page body is an empty SPA shell for non-JS fetchers.
4. **Make the search-dock examples match real query patterns.** The four
   `landing.search.examples` are close to Cluster B sector queries already;
   tune them toward the highest-frequency sector families found in incumbent category
   pages (pulizie/facility, edilizia/manutenzione, informatica, ingegneria) so the
   teaser reads like the queries users already type elsewhere.
5. **First two blog posts = Cluster C + D heads:** "Come compilare il DGUE (e perché
   non dovresti farlo a mano)" and "Partecipare a una gara d'appalto senza ufficio
   gare". Both have proven evergreen demand, weak product-side competition, and land
   exactly on the find→prepare→win narrative.
